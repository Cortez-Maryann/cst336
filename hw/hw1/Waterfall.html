The Waterfall Model is a process seen as flowing steadily downwards through the phases of software
implementation. This means that any phase in the development process begins only if the previous
phase is complete. The waterfall approach does not define the process to go back to the previous phase
to handle changes in requirement. The waterfall approach is the earliest approach and most widely
known that was used for software development. The waterfall approach is the earliest approach and most
